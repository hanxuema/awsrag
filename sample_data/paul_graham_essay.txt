How to Make Wealth
If you wanted to get rich, how would you do it? I think your best bet would be to start or join a startup. That has been a reliable way to get rich for hundreds of years. The word "startup" dates from the 1960s, but what happens in one is identical to what happened in the voyages of mercenary traders in the Middle Ages.

Startups usually involve technology, so much so that "high-tech" and "startup" are almost synonymous. But the technology is not the essential thing. What's essential is that a startup is a small group of people who are solving a difficult, high-leverage problem.

To make wealth, you need to solve a problem that people want solved. You have to create value. There is a common misconception that wealth is a fixed pie, and that for one person to get richer, someone else has to get poorer. This is false. Wealth is what people want—it's food, clothing, shelter, cars, and software. Wealth is not money. Money is just a medium of exchange. You can create more wealth by making things people want.

A startup is a way to compress your working life. Instead of working at a conventional rate for 40 years, you work like crazy for 4 years to create a massive amount of wealth. This compression is possible because:
1. You have high measurement: your individual contribution can be measured.
2. You have high leverage: the decisions you make have a huge multiplier effect.

If you are one of ten employees in a startup, your decisions matter. If you work 10 times harder, the startup succeeds. In a large company, your effort is diluted by thousands of other people. That's why startups are the best way to generate wealth: they give you the leverage and measurement that big companies cannot.
